Lore Entries

The Lavender Town Frequency

One of the most infamous pieces of Pokémon lore. Rumors spread that the original Lavender Town music in Pokémon Red & Green had frequencies that caused dizziness, headaches, and worse in children. Though debunked, this legend lives on in fan culture.

Arceus and the Sinnoh Creation Myth

In the lore of the Sinnoh region, Arceus is said to have emerged from an egg in a place where there was nothing. It shaped the world and created Dialga, Palkia, and Giratina. This myth closely parallels real-world creation stories from many cultures.

The Ghost Girl of Lumiose

In Pokémon X & Y, a mysterious ghost girl appears in Lumiose City’s building, glides across the floor, says “No, you’re not the one…” and disappears. To this day, she has no backstory or explanation in the game.

Phantom Marowak of Pokémon Tower

The ghost of a Marowak haunts Lavender Town's Pokémon Tower. She was killed by Team Rocket while trying to protect her Cubone, and her restless soul lingers until the player sets her free. One of the most emotional moments in early Pokémon games.

Mimikyu's True Form

Mimikyu wears a Pikachu costume because its real form is said to cause a "mysterious illness or death" to those who see it. According to the Pokédex, only researchers who died tragic deaths ever recorded their findings.

AZ’s Floette

In Pokémon X & Y, AZ's special Floette was revived using the ultimate weapon. It's different from any other Floette, and fans theorize it represents eternal sorrow and the price of war. It’s unobtainable in-game, adding to its mystery.
